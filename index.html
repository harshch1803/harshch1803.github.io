Harsh Chaudhari

PhD Student at Northeastern University.

I am a second-year Computer Science PhD Student at Northeastern University, where I am advised by Alina Oprea. My research interests broadly lie at the intersection of machine learning, security, and privacy. Current research focuses on designing attacks on machine learning models to understand and mitigate threats to participating users. Previous research contributions were mainly in the area of privacy-preserving machine learning via Multi-Party Computation (MPC).

I completed my Masters from Indian Institute of Science (IISc) in 2020, under the supervision of Arpita Patra. I spent the summer of 2020 interning with the crypto group at IBM Research Bangalore, where I was hosted by Dhinakaran Vinayagamurthy and Pankaj Dyama.
